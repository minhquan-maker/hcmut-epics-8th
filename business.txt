BUSINESS MODEL CANVAS - AQUAGUARD

Customer Segments

AquaGuard aims to support multiple users which involved in flood disaster prevention and response

Our primary users are residents in flood-damaged areas who require real-time, accurate information to ensure their safety and make critical decisions during emergencies.

Institutional users include government disaster management authorities. who use the platform to monitor, coordinate, and make decisions during disasters.

	Additionally, supporting users such as NGOs and community organizations play a key role in deploying the system, supporting affected populations, and facilitating communication between communities and authorities.
  
Value Proposition
AquaGuard delivers a comprehensive and reliable disaster management solution by combining real-time data, community input, and location-based services.
The platform provides location-based flood alerts, enabling users to receive timely warnings specific to their area. It also supports real-time incident reporting, allowing users to share on-ground information that enhances situational awareness.
Through SOS emergency communication, users can request immediate assistance, while interactive flood maps offer visual insights into affected areas. In addition, the platform provides safety guidance and evacuation information, helping users make informed decisions during critical situations.
Together, these features create a system that improves response speed, accuracy, and overall disaster preparedness.
Channels
AquaGuard utilizes a multi-channel approach to reach and serve its users effectively.
The primary delivery channel is the mobile application. To increase awareness and engagement, the platform leverages social media platforms, enabling rapid information dissemination and community interaction.
It also integrates with government disaster alert systems to ensure reliability and wider reach, especially for users who may not directly access the app.
Furthermore, NGO networks act as important distribution channels, supporting local deployment, user onboarding, and community-level engagement.
Customer Relationships

	AquaGuard is built on a trusted and interactive relationship model, which is essential in high-risk disaster contexts.

	The platform operates on a self-service model with high dependence, where users can independently access critical information but must rely heavily on its accuracy and reliability.
It enables real-time two-way interaction, allowing users to send SOS signals and report incidents while receiving immediate updates.
	Users are also engaged as data contributors, enhancing system accuracy through real-time reports and shared information.
To ensure sustainability, AquaGuard focuses on building long-term relationships, maintaining trust through consistent performance, protecting data privacy, and continuously improving its systems.
Revenue Stream
AquaGuard adopts a hybrid revenue model that combines public funding and strategic partnerships.
Primary revenue sources include government disaster management programs, which fund the platform as part of public safety initiatives.
Additional funding is obtained through grants from NGOs and international organizations, particularly those focused on climate resilience and disaster response.
The platform may also generate revenue through partnerships with technology providers and through optional premium analytics services offered to authorities for advanced data insights and decision support.
Key Resource

	The effectiveness of AquaGuard depends on several critical resources.
Real-time meteorological data serves as the foundation for accurate alerts and predictions. The platform is supported by a cloud-based infrastructure that enables real-time processing, scalability, and system reliability.
User-generated reports provide valuable on-ground insights, complementing official data sources and improving responsiveness.
The system also relies on a geolocation and mapping framework, which enables location-based alerts and supports rescue coordination.
Finally, a technical development team is essential for building, maintaining and continuously improving the platform

	AquaGuard collaborates with multiple stakeholders to ensure operational effectiveness and scalability.
Key partners include government authorities that support implementation and policy integration, and meteorological agencies that provide essential environmental data.
NGOs play a crucial role in community engagement and local deployment, while technology providers support infrastructure and system development.

AquaGuard’s cost structure is primarily driven by technology development and operational requirements.
